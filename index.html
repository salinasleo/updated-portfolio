About my web development journey:


My 7 yr old boy likes videogames and wants me to build games for him. I told him it takes teams to build the apps he's addicted to, but here I am anyway giving it a shot. I enrolled into a coding bootcamp at the University of Richmond. I took every creative liberty we had in our assignments to try to build something he would hopefully enjoy!

  • As part of the program prework I built him a game in Scratch where a cat kills bats. He loved that the cat shot an image of him as weapon, but suggested I change the enemy from bats to mice so I showed him how to do that. I also had to change the difficulty level because he got frustrated by fast moving mice!
  • For a Javascript assignment I built this Jelly Bean Collector Game! He did enjoy that for a little while, he loves math.
  • As part of the Firebase homework I built a Rock Paper Scissors Spock multiplayer game for him and we played it simultaneously on different laptops. It was great to learn about cloud databases.
  • For a React assignment, I built him a " Yu Gi Oh Themed Memory Game. To me all the characters look the same, but he names them as he's clicking the characters, and quickly got to the maximum score of 12. I then increased the images to 18 and he got 18 almost right away. Then I increased it to 24 and he got to 22 of 24 and then his attention went elsewhere. I was so glad when he asked to play the game again the next day :)
This site itself is an assignment in the use of html, css, and javascript (hover or click over my profile picture).

Before the bootcamp I created an e-commerce website using Volusion.com for a business I started in 2017. I intend to copy it locally and build on it, for example I want to add the ability to take cryptocurrency as payment.

For my dayjob I do a lot of analytics for SunTrust Bank, where I have been able to analyze and summarize our loan performance, default operations, fraud operations, customer satisfaction, etc. Feel free to check out and connect with me on LinkedIn.
